ScoMo makes his first big decision

By: Zack N

Summary: Scott Morrison makes announces a major policy change on breakfast television which the opposition government has been pushing for years.



Australia has an “Age Pension”, which is designed to provide a form of income to old people when they are unable to work. To qualify for the aged pension, among other things, you need to be of a certain age.



When the pension was introduced in 1910, the pension age was 65, and life expectancy was around 58. That is, most people would actually die before being old enough to receive a pension. As a result, only 100,000 people were receiving it.



Fast forward to today, and our life expectancy is 82 years. As a result, there are 2.2 million people on the pension.



Recently, there has been a lot of change regarding the pension age. Here’s a quick summary:


  • In 2009, the Rudd government announced it would increase the pension age from 65 to 67 from 2017-2023 (six months every 2 years).
  • In 2014, under the Abbott government, Treasurer Joe Hockey announced the pension age would continue increasing up to 70 years old.
  • Labour has been campaigning against this change ever since.
  • Last Wednesday, Morrison announced it would stay at 67 in 2023, not increasing to 70.
  • The pension age is currently 65 years and six months.


    • The issues surrounding this are complex. While the pension age should theoretically increase in order to compensate for increasing life expectancies (keeping people in the labour force for longer), people simply don’t want to work until they are 70 years old, especially those in physically demanding jobs — 70 year-olds don’t mix well with lifting heavy things for some reason.



      The issue itself aside, an important question is, “Why now?” Scott Morrison has been the treasurer for years and hasn’t changed this. To us, This seems like a populist move from a desperate government. As Bill Shorten put it, Morrison seems to be “temporarily borrowing Labour policy”.



      So much for shifting the liberal party further to the right.



      And, um, why did he have to announce it on the Today Show?
