Liberal Agendas

Summary: In the past week, the Liberal party have voted for a lot of things; the privatisation of the ABC, and moving an embassy to Jerusalem from Tel Aviv.



100 Liberal Party MP’s flocked to Sydney to attend the annual Liberal Party federal council, to discuss their plans for the year ahead. Specifically, at the event, they targeted two issues . The privatisation of the ABC , and the movement of the Tel Aviv Australian Embassy to Jerusalem. Let’s break these two down.



1. The Privatisation of the ABC



The Australian Broadcasting Commision (ABC) is currently funded by the government, although it received funding freezes in the most recent budget. The Liberal Party wants to remove this funding entirely and separate it from the government. This would make it a private entity.



This move would free up a fair bit of money for the government, but could possibly force the ABC to cut some positions or perhaps even become slightly biased due to a for-profit organisation funding it, just like we have seen in news sources in the USA (Fox, CNN, etc..



2. The movement of the Australian embassy to Jerusalem



The Liberal Government wanted to also move the Australian Embassy that is in Tel Aviv to Jerusalem. This comes after Donald Trump moved the USA embassy to Jerusalem in a highly controversial manner.



Julie Bishop, the Foreign Minister for Australia, said that “Jerusalem is a final status issue” and that “[Australia] has maintained that position for decades.” Regardless, it wasn’t passed (43 votes to 37).



Although the Liberal Party voted for these changes, they aren’t binding to the government, so they probably aren’t going to affect the government’s current rules.
