MP exposes major Chinese “donor”

Summary: Liberal MP, Andrew Hastie revealed in a public Parliament session that Dr. Chau Chak Wing allegedly co-conspired to bribe the President of the United Nations General Assembly.



Before we get into the story, it is important to say that Hastie had special Parliamentary rights when saying this. If a normal person were to say this in the media, they would be sued for defamation.



Some context, Chau Chak Wing is a Chinese Australian who has allegedly donated $4 MILLION to both major political parties. Obviously, as an Australian citizen, this is perfectly legal if not a little bit over the top.



Last Tuesday, Andrew Hastie gave a speech about his recent intelligence meeting with the FBI. In the speech, he essentially revealed that in a recent law case involving the attempted bribery of the President of the UN General Assembly, one of the co-conspirators kept anonymous as CC-3 is actually Chau Chak Wing.



Wow, no one would have expected that. Someone donating suspiciously large amounts to political parties is also trying to “donate” to other powerful figures.



Hastie says that exposing this information is his duty to Australia which justifies his actions.

Dogshot by-elections

Summary: Turnbull’s pulled a fast one on the Labor Party by calling the by-elections for 5 electorates on the day of the ALP’s National Conference.



He’s done it again. Turnbull got his political sniper, and pulled the trigger. Except this time, it’s on the Labor Party, not Tony Abbot.



He has called the by-elections for the electorates of Perth, Fremantle, Mayo, Longman and Braddon (the electorates where MPs resigned either due to personal reasons or dual citizenship) on the day of Labor’s national conference.



This means that many of the top Labor party representatives will not be able to go to the convention to talk to their supporters but instead will have to go out to the election areas to support their candidates.



As for the actual elections, Labor are almost certain to have Perth and Fremantle, as the Liberals are not running in those areas.



Braddon and Longman are in contention as the former Labor MPs who were disqualified due to dual citizenship are attempting to run again. In past, these seats were barely won by Labor so it may be a tough battle for them to win them again.



Lastly, the electorate of Mayo is likely going to be won by the Liberals.



As a side note, Australia has some pretty weird electorate names. Mayo, Batman, Balaclava and Diamond Valley are some notable ones.
